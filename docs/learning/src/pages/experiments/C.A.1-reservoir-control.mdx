## The bet (Source-(iv))

The reservoir family bets on the structural [loss-per-FLOP](/concepts/loss-per-flop-and-scaling-laws)
edge: a **fixed random recurrent core is free to train** (frozen ⇒ zero backward pass), so the only
"learning" cost is a **linear readout**. If a rich enough random memory plus a cheap readout could match
the transformer's learned controller, you would get the bar's skill at a fraction of the backward FLOPs.
Two variants test two ways to pay for the readout — distill it offline (C.A.1), or grow it online at eval
(C.A.1b).

## Intuition — an echo-state network

A **reservoir** (echo-state network) is a large pool of randomly-wired recurrent units. You never train
the recurrence; you train only a linear readout off its state. The reservoir's whole job is to be a rich,
*fading* memory: its state $h_t$ is a nonlinear echo of the recent input trajectory, so a linear readout
can pick off whatever feature it needs. On the [chemotaxis tape](/concepts/in-context-control),
"in-context adaptation" is just the reservoir **state** echoing the recent sense/act history — there is no
weight change at eval, exactly analogous to a transformer carrying its [KV
cache](/concepts/fast-weight-memory) as memory.

## The math — frozen core, trained readout (C.A.1)

$$h_t = (1-\lambda)\,h_{t-1} + \lambda \tanh\!\big(W_{in}[:,\text{token}] + W_{res}\,h_{t-1}\big), \qquad \text{logits} = W_{out}\,h_t + b$$

$W_{in}$ and $W_{res}$ are **frozen** random matrices (spectral radius $\rho = 0.9$ for the echo-state
property) and are **counted in `num_params`** for memory parity — but they form no autograd graph, so they
cost **zero backward**. Per token, the forward pass is the $W_{res}\,h$ matvec ($2 d_{res}^2$, the dominant
term) plus the readout ($2\,V d_{res}$) plus pointwise ops; the backward pass is **only** the readout's
$dW_{out},\,db_{out}$ — not the usual 2×-forward tax, because the frozen recurrence has no gradient. Cost is
$O(d_{res}^2)$ per step and **context-independent** (versus the transformer's $O(n_{\text{layers}} d^2 +
\text{context}\cdot d)$).

<Callout variant="note" title="sizing for parity">
$d_{res} = 374 \Rightarrow$ **148,115 params** $\le$ the bar's 148,608 ($W_{res}$ alone is 139,876). Only
**~4,125 params — the readout — are trainable.** Same parameter budget as the transformer; a sliver of it
learns.
</Callout>

## The plastic twist — learning at eval (C.A.1b)

C.A.1b keeps the same frozen core but makes the readout **plastic**: a working copy of $(W, b)$ lives in
the decode state and adapts **online during the rollout** via gradient-free local rules — so distillation
is ≈ zero and essentially **all "learning" happens at eval**, inside `step`, counted FLOP-for-FLOP. Two
rules fire once the reward and target are observable:

- **World model** (concentration columns) — an online softmax **delta** rule: $W[\text{conc}] \leftarrow W[\text{conc}] + \eta_{wm}\,(\text{onehot}(c) - \text{softmax}(\hat c)) \otimes h_{\text{action}}$.
- **Policy** (action columns) — **reward-modulated Hebbian** with a leaky baseline, reward proxy $r = c/(L-1)$: $W[\text{action}] \leftarrow W[\text{action}] + \eta_{pol}\,(r - \bar r)\,\text{onehot}(a_{\text{taken}}) \otimes h_{\text{conc}}$.

A fast baseline makes $(r - \bar r) \approx \Delta\text{concentration}$, turning the policy rule into an
approximate **online run-and-tumble learner**. Every update FLOP is charged to `step.backward`
(cross-vendor audited); the rules are gradient-free, local, and $O(d_{res}^2)$ per step.

<Callout variant="caveat" title="param parity, not all-memory parity">
`num_params` is **148,115** — param-axis parity with the bar. But the plastic readout's eval working copy
adds **~4k floats of eval state**, analogous to the transformer's KV cache / [fast-weight
memory](/concepts/fast-weight-memory) (also excluded from `num_params`). State it honestly: this is
parameter-axis parity, **not** full all-memory parity.
</Callout>

**C.A.1 — distilled-frozen readout.** Regret falls monotonically with compute (the loss-per-FLOP discipline
holds), but it bottoms out **above** the bar:

| distill steps | total FLOPs | regret ↓ | reward ↑ | wm bits |
| ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 150  | $9.29\times10^{10}$ | 0.494 | 0.439 | 1.59 |
| 600  | $3.68\times10^{11}$ | 0.371 | 0.562 | 1.38 |
| 1500 | $9.18\times10^{11}$ | 0.278 | 0.655 | 1.29 |

At $9.18\times10^{11}$ FLOPs — **3× the bar's cheapest budget** ($2.97\times10^{11}$) — the reservoir
reaches only **0.278 regret**, still worse than the bar's **0.229**. The structural backward-FLOP edge is
real, but a linear readout over a fixed random reservoir is a weaker controller than the transformer; the
**fixed representation caps the achievable regret**.

**C.A.1b — online-plastic readout.** At ~0 distillation it costs only $1.22\times10^{9}$ total FLOPs —
**~243× cheaper** than the bar's cheapest point — and it genuinely learns *within the episode*: the
second-half reward beats the first, and both clear the random floor.

Its headline regret is **0.501** — ~2.2× the bar's 0.229. So C.A.1b occupies a distinct **ultra-cheap /
modest-skill** regime: real Source-(iv) online learning, but well short of the lower-left.

<Callout variant="caveat" title="honest robustness of the online signal">
Floor-beating is **robust**: +0.05…+0.09 reward across horizons {16, 32, 64} × seeds {0, 1, 2}. The
*within-episode* 2nd-&gt;1st-half signal is positive at seed 0 but **seed-sensitive under env drift** — a
documented honesty caveat, not a cherry-pick. The online world model stays poor (wm bits ≈ **23**); it is
the **reward/policy** that improves, not prediction.
</Callout>

## Verdict — LOSES on regret (both variants)

Both reservoir controllers are **honest negatives** on the headline regret-per-FLOP metric. C.A.1 caps
above the bar; C.A.1b is genuinely-online but modest. A fixed random reservoir plus a linear readout
underperforms the transformer's learned representation on control.

## What we learned

1. **A fixed random reservoir caps achievable regret.** The frozen core buys a real backward-FLOP
   discount, but its representation is not expressive enough for a *linear* readout to match the
   transformer's controller — the discount cannot overcome the ceiling.
2. **Genuine online learning is real, but modest here.** C.A.1b learns within the episode at ~0
   distillation (2nd-half &gt; 1st-half, clears the floor) — the purest Source-(iv) dynamics in the suite —
   yet its skill plateaus well above the bar.
3. **Plot the floor first (again).** Both variants clear the random floor and *look* like learning, but on
   the per-FLOP regret axis the bar still wins. It is the [Space-B Pareto-hollow
   reflex](/experiments/first-finding-pareto) carried onto control — and the next page shows a cheap, fixed,
   hand-tuned reaction taking the metric outright.
