## Why this rung — the C.A.0 gap, fixed

`ChemoEnv`'s optimal policy is "climb the local gradient." A rate-agnostic reactor clears every held-out
drift rate without inferring anything, so the rung never separated a *learner* from a clever fixed
reaction — the [`chemotaxis_min`](/experiments/C.A.2-chemotaxis-min-control) result was exactly this
embarrassment. `ForageEnv` makes the optimum depend on a **latent the agent cannot know a priori**, so
the only thing that generalizes is the *inference algorithm* — "eat the type that pays, skip the type
that poisons."

## The environment — `ForageEnv`

A stationary ring of $W = 16$ cells. **Every cell holds a cue of one of $K = 3$ types**, sampled
i.i.d. uniform at episode start and **fixed thereafter**. Exactly one type $g$ is **good** this episode,
drawn uniform per episode; $g$ and the layout are **fresh every episode** (train/eval seed bands
disjoint), so there is **no fixed mapping to memorize**. The agent at position $p$ senses only its own
cell's type. Actions are `LEFT`, `EAT`, `RIGHT`.

- **`EAT` eats in place** (the agent does not move; the cell is re-eatable): reward $+1$ if the cell's
  type $== g$, else $-1$ — **poison**. `LEFT`/`RIGHT` move without eating (reward $0$).

### The math — why these exact dynamics

Two properties are load-bearing, and **both were Monte-Carlo verified**:

- **Poison ($-1$) makes blind eating net-negative.** Eat without knowing $g$ and the expected reward is
  $\frac{1 - (K-1)}{K} = \frac{2-K}{K} = -\tfrac13$ at $K=3$. So a weightless `always-EAT` **cannot** win
  — the rung is **reflex-proof**.
- **Stationary cells + eat-in-place make camping $g$ the optimum.** Once the agent identifies a $g$ cell,
  staying and eating it yields $+1$ **every step** — the per-step ceiling. That makes the **$g$-knowing
  oracle a valid upper bound** (regret $\ge 0$), while food **never depletes** (cells never change), so a
  learner can improve *within* the episode — the rung is **distillable**.

The obs symbol encodes **both** the current cue type **and** the last reward —
$\text{obs} = \text{type}\cdot 3 + (\text{last\_reward}+1)$, with $\text{last\_reward}\in\{-1,0,+1\}$ — so
the policy always sees the current type *and* the reward signal stays in-context, on the **same 2-token**
`obs · action · obs · action` tape chemotaxis uses (vocab $= 3K+3 = 12$: obs $0\ldots3K{-}1$, actions
above). No scorer-schedule change.

## The env-design lesson — "reflex-proof" is a property you must *prove*

Getting a reflex-proof **and** non-depleting **and** valid-oracle environment is hard, and adversarial
review caught **two would-have-shipped-broken designs** — both with the *same* failure as C.A.0
(a fixed reflex beats the "oracle," so regret goes negative and measures nothing):

<Callout variant="caveat" title="two broken designs, both caught by review">
1. **First dynamics (reward $\{0, +1\}$, `EAT` refreshes the cell to a random type).** The **4-lens spec
   review** Monte-Carlo-proved that `always-EAT` **farms the refresh** at rate $1/K$ and *beats* the
   oracle → **negative regret** (C.A.0's failure again). **Fix: poison $-1$** so blind eating is
   net-negative.
2. **An `EAT`-advances variant.** The **cross-vendor codex (gpt-5.5) review** caught a **bounce-camp**
   exploit — eat $g$, step `LEFT` back onto it, eat again — that beats the traverse-oracle, making the
   regret reference **invalid**. **Fix: `EAT` eats in place** (re-eatable, no movement).

Both fixes were MC-verified. The lesson generalizes: **reflex-proof is a property you must *prove* by
Monte-Carlo, not assert** — exactly the discipline C.A.0 lacked.
</Callout>

## Reference policies — the reflex is *below* random, the source approaches the oracle

Monte-Carlo mean per-step reward ($W=16$, $K=3$, $H=64$), across the policy family. This is the headline
that says the rung works: the blind reflex (`always_eat`) sits **below random**, while the
within-episode learner (`win_stay_lose_shift`) climbs nearly to the $g$-knowing oracle.

## The fair baseline — the bar is the best of a sweep, not one config

A transformer distill-train on this rung is **fast** (≈ 15 s – 2 min) and regret **plateaus on FLOPs
alone** — so *tuning, not compute, is the lever*. An un-swept single config would be a strawman; the
honest bar is the **best of a training-hyperparameter sweep at fixed params**. A **36-config** sweep
(lr × weight_decay × batch × $\varepsilon$) gave regret ranging from **0.15 to 0.65**:

<Callout variant="insight" title="tuning, not compute, is the lever">
The 4× regret spread across hyperparameters at fixed params and budget — while the FLOP curve is **flat**
— is the whole reason the bar must be the best of a sweep. Reporting one arbitrary config could have
quietly handed a candidate a 0.5-regret strawman to beat. The chosen **0.16** is the honest target a
local-learning candidate must clear.
</Callout>

## The realized `Environment` seam

This rung also paid down infrastructure debt: `evaluate_control`, `make_distillation_batch`, and
`distill_train_run` were **hardcoded** to `ChemoEnv`. They now run on a small **`EnvSpec`** bundle
(`env_factory` + `source_factory` + a `tape_spec` of vocab/slices), so:

Chemotaxis is **instance #1**, forage **#2**, and chemotaxis was kept **bit-identical** — a new
end-to-end pin test asserts its regret / reward / world-model bits / FLOPs are unchanged after the
refactor (drift would be invisible to FLOPs otherwise). New rungs now drop in by shipping an
`Environment` + its `EnvSpec` — no scorer or harness surgery.

## What we learned

1. **Reflex-proof must be proven, not assumed.** Two plausible designs each let a fixed reflex beat the
   oracle (negative regret); only Monte-Carlo proof — across the whole fixed-policy family — established
   that poison + eat-in-place actually require in-context learning.
2. **The bar is a sweep, not a config.** With regret flat on FLOPs but spanning 0.15–0.65 on
   hyperparameters, the only honest baseline is the best of a fixed-params sweep — anything else is a
   strawman a candidate could beat without merit.
3. **Generalize the seam once, then rungs are cheap.** Realizing the `Environment`/`EnvSpec` protocol
   (chemotaxis kept bit-identical by a pin test) means the *next* rung — and the local-learning
   candidate scored on this one — is a drop-in, not a refactor.

<Callout variant="note" title="this rung exists for the prize built next">
Forage is **infrastructure and a baseline**, not a result to celebrate. Its purpose is to be the first
control rung a *genuine in-context learner* must win — the [Source-(iv)](/concepts/source-iv-advantage)
local-learning candidate is built and scored against this **0.16** bar next, the way C.A.1/C.A.2
challenged the chemotaxis bar.
</Callout>
